========================================================= FORENSIQ
INVESTIGATION FILE Case ID: FIQ-2026-114 Classification: Confidential
=========================================================

Incident Date: 9 February 2026

Incident Time: Approximately 17:30 (last seen); ransom call received
20:10

Location: St. Augustine's School, Kondapur, Hyderabad (last seen) and
Residence of the Malhotra family, Jubilee Hills, Hyderabad

Missing Person: Aarav Malhotra, age 9, Class 4 student

  -------------------
  WITNESS STATEMENT
  -------------------

My name is Ritu Malhotra. I am Aarav's mother.

Aarav usually returns home by school van at 16:45. On 9 February, the
van arrived at our building gate at the usual time, but our driver,
Suresh Pawar, told me Aarav was not on the van when it left school.

I immediately called the school. The front office said Aarav had been
picked up early at 15:50 by a man who presented an authorization slip
claiming to be sent by us, signed with what appeared to be my
husband's signature.

I did not send anyone to collect Aarav, and my husband was in a
meeting from 14:00 to 18:00 with three colleagues who can confirm his
presence throughout.

At 20:10, we received a call on my husband's personal mobile number
demanding ₹50,00,000 for Aarav's safe return, with instructions to not
involve the police.

  --------------------------
  SECOND WITNESS STATEMENT
  --------------------------

My name is Suresh Pawar. I am the school van driver for the
Malhotra family and three other families on the same route.

I arrived at the school gate at 15:45 as usual to wait for pickup
time. I did not see Aarav leave early. I assumed he was inside until
the regular dismissal at 15:50, at which point the front office staff
told me he had "already left with a guardian."

I have driven this route for six years and know Aarav by sight. I did
not see who picked him up, as I was parked at the rear gate, not the
front entrance where pickups are normally verified.

I want to mention that two weeks ago, I was asked by Mr. Malhotra's
brother, Mr. Vikas Malhotra, whether I could "make an exception" and
drop Aarav at a different address one day, for a "family event." I
declined, as this was against company policy, and I did not report
this to Mrs. Malhotra at the time.

  ---------------------------
  THIRD WITNESS STATEMENT
  ---------------------------

My name is Father Thomas D'Souza. I am the school's administrative
head.

The authorization slip presented at 15:50 bore a signature resembling
Mr. Rohan Malhotra's, along with a photocopy of a family ID card. Our
front desk staff, who are new and had not previously verified the
Malhotra family's authorized contacts, accepted it without calling
the registered parent numbers, which is against our standard
procedure.

The man who presented the slip was described as approximately 40
years old, of medium build, wearing a grey shirt, and arrived in a
white sedan. He did not enter the building and waited near the gate.

Our gate CCTV captured the white sedan but not its number plate
clearly, due to glare. The man's face was partially visible but not
identifiable with certainty.

Organizations - St. Augustine's School, Kondapur - Malhotra Family

Evidence - Forged authorization slip with photocopied ID - Partial
CCTV footage showing white sedan and unidentifiable male, gate camera
only - Ransom call recorded on Mr. Malhotra's phone, voice
digitally distorted, lasting 47 seconds - Phone used for ransom call
traced to a prepaid SIM activated using fake documents three days
prior, signal triangulated to the Kondapur-Gachibowli corridor at
time of call - Property dispute documents showing Vikas Malhotra
contesting a will that left the majority share of the family's
ancestral property to Rohan Malhotra, currently in mediation -
Bank records showing Vikas Malhotra withdrew ₹3,00,000 in cash four
days before the incident, citing "personal expenses" - A second ransom
call received at 22:45, this time demanding the money be delivered to
a location near Shamshabad, with the caller referencing details about
Aarav's daily school routine that only someone close to the family
would know

Possible Contradiction Mr. Pawar states he did not report Vikas
Malhotra's earlier request to anyone, which delayed this information
reaching investigators by two weeks. Separately, Vikas Malhotra, when
questioned, states he made the request to Mr. Pawar "as a joke" and
denies any involvement, claiming his cash withdrawal was for an
unrelated land deposit, which has not yet been verified.

The man described at the school gate was of "medium build, approximately
40," a description that does not clearly match either Vikas Malhotra
(reported as tall and broad) or any other named individual, raising
the possibility of a third party acting on someone else's behalf.

Unresolved Question The ransom caller's knowledge of Aarav's routine
suggests insider information, but it remains unclear whether this
points to Vikas Malhotra directly, someone he hired, or an unrelated
individual with separate access to the family's schedule, such as
household staff not yet interviewed.
