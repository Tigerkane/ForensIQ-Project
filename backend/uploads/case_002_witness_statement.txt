========================================================= FORENSIQ
INVESTIGATION FILE Case ID: FIQ-2026-077 Classification: Confidential
=========================================================

Incident Date: 12 March 2026

Incident Time: Approximately 22:10

Location: Greenfield Heights Apartments, Tower C, Floor 9, Banjara Hills,
Hyderabad

  -------------------
  WITNESS STATEMENT
  -------------------

My name is Meera Kapoor. I live in Flat 9B, directly across the
corridor from Flat 9C.

At approximately 21:50, I heard raised voices coming from Flat 9C,
which is occupied by Mr. Vikram Saxena. I recognized one of the voices
as belonging to his business partner, Mr. Deepak Oberoi, who visits
frequently.

The argument seemed to be about money. I distinctly heard Mr. Oberoi
say, "You promised me the shares would be transferred by Friday."

Around 22:05, the voices stopped. I assumed the argument had ended and
went back to watching television.

At approximately 22:15, I heard a loud crash, as if furniture had been
overturned, followed by complete silence.

I opened my door at around 22:20 and saw Mr. Oberoi leaving Flat 9C in
visible distress. His shirt appeared to have a dark stain near the
collar. He did not notice me and took the stairs instead of the lift.

I did not enter Flat 9C myself. I called the building security desk at
22:25.

Security entered the flat at 22:31 and found Mr. Vikram Saxena
unresponsive on the living room floor. Police were called immediately.

  --------------------------
  SECOND WITNESS STATEMENT
  --------------------------

My name is Farhan Ali.

I am the on-duty lift operator for Tower C.

I clearly recall operating the lift for Mr. Oberoi at 22:22, taking him
down from the 9th floor to the ground floor lobby.

He did not take the stairs. He used the lift, and I remember this
specifically because he asked me to hurry and seemed agitated.

I also noticed his shirt was clean, with no visible stains, though his
hands appeared to be shaking.

I did not see anyone else come down from the 9th floor between 21:45
and 22:31, when security was called up.

  INVESTIGATION NOTES
  ---------------------

Known Individuals - Meera Kapoor (Neighbor, Flat 9B) - Vikram Saxena
(Deceased, Flat 9C, Business Owner) - Deepak Oberoi (Business Partner
of Deceased) - Farhan Ali (Lift Operator, Tower C) - Building Security
Staff (Names withheld pending interview)

Organizations - Saxena Oberoi Ventures Pvt. Ltd. (Joint venture between
deceased and Mr. Oberoi)

Cause of Death (Preliminary) Blunt force trauma to the head, consistent
with impact against a heavy object. Time of death estimated between
22:05 and 22:20.

Evidence - Overturned glass coffee table, shattered - Bloodstain on
edge of marble side table - Victim's mobile phone, screen cracked,
last call logged at 21:48 to Mr. Oberoi - Shareholder transfer
agreement, partially signed, found on dining table - Single set of
wet shoe prints leading from balcony door to living room - Balcony
door found unlocked; flat is on the 9th floor with no adjoining
balcony access - Security desk call log confirming Ms. Kapoor's call
at 22:25 - CCTV at ground floor lobby entrance malfunctioning since
14 March (data unavailable for that night)

Financial Activity A wire transfer of ₹1,20,00,000 was initiated from
Saxena Oberoi Ventures Pvt. Ltd. to a personal account belonging to Mr.
Oberoi at 21:40, twenty-five minutes before the recorded phone call to
the deceased. The transfer was later found to be unauthorized, as it
required Mr. Saxena's digital signature, which had not been provided.

Possible Contradiction Ms. Kapoor states that Mr. Oberoi left Flat 9C
at approximately 22:20 and took the stairs, with a visible stain on
his shirt. Mr. Ali states that he personally operated the lift for
Mr. Oberoi at 22:22, and that Mr. Oberoi's shirt was clean. Mr. Ali also
states no one else used the lift from the 9th floor that night, and
the lobby CCTV that could resolve this is non-functional.

Unresolved Question The wet shoe prints found near the balcony door
are inconsistent with weather conditions that night, as no rainfall
was recorded in the area. Source of the moisture has not yet been
determined. The balcony has no external access point, raising the
possibility of an unaccounted entry or exit route.
